Feedback on My Website

Recently, I got some really useful feedback on the website I’ve been working on, and I’m excited to share both the things I’ve improved and the compliments I received. The feedback session was a great way for me to see what was working well and what could be improved, so I can make my website even better.

One of the most helpful pieces of feedback was about organizing my code. I was told that I should sort my code better, especially to make it easier for others to understand if they look at it. To fix this, I went through the HTML and CSS files and made sure everything was neatly arranged. This helped me feel more confident that my code is clean and easier to follow.

Another suggestion was to add more comments to my code. While I had some comments here and there, I realized I wasn’t explaining enough of what each section did. By adding more comments, I now have clearer explanations, which will make future updates easier for me and anyone else who might work on the code.

I also got feedback on the design side. One point was to make my footer bigger, so it balances better with the rest of the page. After tweaking the size and layout, it looks much more polished. Someone else suggested adding an aside bar for extra content or navigation, which was something I hadn’t thought of before, but I’m glad I added it because it really enhances the user experience.

While I got a lot of suggestions for improvement, I also received a lot of compliments! People really liked the color scheme I used, saying it was easy on the eyes and looked professional. I was also praised for the responsive design—my site works well on both desktop and mobile, which was something I worked hard on. Another thing people liked was the overall layout. The navigation is simple and easy to use, which is exactly what I was aiming for.

All in all, this feedback session was really helpful. It showed me what I did well and what I could still improve. The changes I’ve made have made my website better, and I’m looking forward to continuing to make it even more user-friendly and visually appealing.
